Mandombe

  • The Mandombe alphabet was devised in 1978 by David Wabeladio Payi from the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC).

  • It was apparently revealed in a dream to Simon Kimbangu, the prophet of the Kimbanguist Church.

  • It is based on two shapes thought of as sacred that look like 5 and 2. It is intended to write the national languages of the Congo: Kikongo, Lingala, Tshiluba and Swahili.

Sound Changes

  • All languages change how they’re pronounced over time
  • Think about:
    • which vs. witch
    • caught vs. cot
    • merry vs. mary vs. marry

Sound Changes from Luvian to Greek

  • Wilusa = Ilion

  • In Ancient Greek:

    • The sound /w/ is lost:

      • hydro- = water

      • ops ‘voice’ = vox ‘voice’

    • The sound /s/ becomes [h], and is lost between vowels

      • hex = six

    • Thus:  wiluson > iluon > Ilion
